## How to Read the Analysis

### Pivot Zones

Pivot zones are approximate areas where price action may pause or reverse. They often line up with moving-average clusters, previous highs, previous lows, or liquidity zones.

### Supports and Resistances

**Supports** are areas where buying interest has previously emerged.

**Resistances** are areas where selling pressure has previously appeared.

### Swing Setup

The swing setup outlines whether momentum, mean-reversion, or breakout strategies may be favorable.

### Risk Management

Risk management suggests stop-loss areas and three take-profit points.

Since the focus is more on pivot and reversal areas than tight stop-loss distance, stops are placed slightly beyond key support or resistance zones.

### Take-Profit Structure

- **TP1:** conservative profit target
- **TP2:** moderate profit target
- **TP3:** aggressive profit target

For NFT collections, SOL-denominated values are converted to USD using the SOL price of **$85.76**.

Example: A 20% gain on DeGods’ **5.87 SOL** floor price equals about **7.04 SOL**, or about **$604**.

# Risk Considerations

## Macro Risk

Persistent inflation, rising tariffs, and uncertain Fed policy create headwinds for risk assets.

A resurgence of volatility in the U.S. dollar or a spike in 10-year yields above **5%** could trigger sharp crypto drawdowns.

## Regulatory Risk

Ongoing litigation and evolving tax or tariff policies may impact token liquidity.

Position sizes should remain modest, especially in lower-liquidity tokens.

## Liquidity Risk

Small-cap tokens such as **PENGU, ME, S, SNS, and GRASS** have thin order books.

Most NFT collections also have thin liquidity. Large orders can cause slippage. Limit orders are preferred over market orders.

## Stop-Loss Discipline

Even when pivot zones matter more than tight stop losses, soft stops and alerts around support breaks help manage downside risk.

Stops should be adjusted as trades move in your favor.

## Portfolio Allocation

As a long-term leaps trader, speculative NFTs and micro-cap tokens should remain a modest portion of the portfolio.

More liquid assets such as **BTC, ETH, SOL, or cash equivalents** should remain the core of the risk framework.
